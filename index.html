Baumert Academy

Baumert Academy is a North Carolina–registered nonpublic K–12 homeschool operating pursuant to G.S. 115C-563(a). The Academy maintains compliance with North Carolina Division of Non-Public Education (DNPE) requirements for administration, instruction, attendance, and annual nationally standardized testing.

Instruction is parent-directed with individualized pacing and documented progress. Eligible upper-school students may participate in dual-enrollment opportunities with accredited institutions of higher education.

Institutional Overview

Baumert Academy was established as a privately administered homeschool to provide a structured, supportive learning environment with clear expectations, documented coursework, and regular progress reviews. The school year follows a traditional August–June calendar with flexibility for instructional days and enrichment.

Accreditation: Homeschools in North Carolina are not accredited by the State. Diplomas and transcripts are issued by the school in accordance with DNPE guidance.

Academic Program

K–5

Foundational literacy and numeracy; age-appropriate science and social studies; daily reading.

  • Phonics, handwriting, read-alouds
  • Number sense and arithmetic practice
  • Hands-on activities and review

6–8

Composition, pre-algebra/algebra, general science, world and U.S. history; study skills.

  • Structured writing and projects
  • Organization, note-taking, assessments
  • Regular feedback and revision

9–12

Literature and composition; mathematics through advanced levels as appropriate; laboratory sciences; civics/economics.

  • Documented courses and transcripts
  • Independent reading and research
  • Dual-enrollment where eligible

Enrollment & Records

Attendance, coursework logs, grades, and periodic summaries are maintained for planning and for college/career documentation.

  • Daily attendance and coursework logs
  • Grading scales appropriate to course
  • Quarterly/semester summaries
